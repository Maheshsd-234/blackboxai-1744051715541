The Problem

Real-World Healthcare Pain Points

Current healthcare systems suffer from fragmentation, lack of ownership, and security vulnerabilities.

Data Fragmentation

Your medical history is scattered across multiple hospitals, clinics, and labs. When you visit a new doctor, they can't see your past prescriptions or scans.

No Patient Ownership

Hospitals and software companies own your health data. You have no control over who sees it, no way to revoke access, and no say in how long it's stored.

Lack of Interoperability

Different hospitals use different systems (some still use paper!), making it impossible to share or sync your data easily.

Our Solution

Why Blockchain Is the Perfect Solution

Decentralization

No central authority owns the data — users have full control over their health records.

Security

Using encryption + IPFS + blockchain means the data is encrypted, distributed, and tamper-proof.

Transparency + Trust

All record access is logged. Once a report is added, it can't be altered without leaving a trace.

Ownership

Users can grant or revoke access to their data — even doctors need permission.
