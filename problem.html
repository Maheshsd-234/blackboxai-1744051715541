Healthcare Challenges

Real-World Healthcare Pain Points

Current systems suffer from fragmentation, lack of ownership, and security vulnerabilities.

Data Fragmentation

Your medical history is often scattered across multiple hospitals, clinics, and labs:

  • You visit a new doctor? They can't see your past prescriptions or scans.
  • You change cities? Your records don't follow you.
  • Different specialists? Each has only partial information.

Impact:

Leads to duplicate tests, misdiagnoses, and inefficient care.

No Patient Ownership

Right now, hospitals and software companies own your health data. You have:

  • No control over who sees it
  • No way to revoke access
  • No say in how long it's stored
  • Limited ability to correct errors

Impact:

Creates privacy risks and limits patient autonomy.

Lack of Interoperability

Different healthcare providers use incompatible systems:

  • Proprietary EHR (Electronic Health Record) systems that don't communicate
  • Some institutions still rely on paper records
  • No standardized format for health data exchange

Impact:

Creates barriers to coordinated care and data sharing.

Data Breaches

Medical data is extremely sensitive. Yet, hospitals and apps are regularly hacked:

  • In 2023 alone, over 100 million patient records were leaked globally
  • Centralized databases are attractive targets for hackers
  • Once leaked, data can be used for scams, identity theft, or insurance fraud

Impact:

Exposes patients to financial and personal security risks.

No Trust

Current systems lack verifiable authenticity:

  • How do you know your prescription hasn't been modified?
  • Can you verify the authenticity of your medical report?
  • Is the lab result you're viewing the original or has it been altered?

Impact:

Undermines confidence in healthcare data integrity.
